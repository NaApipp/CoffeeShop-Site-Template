What materials are used?

Arabica Coffee Beans

Arabica coffee, also known as Arabic coffee, Arabic bush coffee, or mountain coffee, is a species of the genus Coffea.

Flores Bajawa Coffee Beans

Flores Bajawa coffee beans have a sweet taste with a balanced level of acidity with a touch of other flavors such as caramel, chocolate, hazelnut, macadamia, and even a herbal taste too.

Bali Kintamani Coffee Beans

With a distinctive taste, namely the aroma of citrus and a low level of acidity. The taste of Bali Kintamani Coffee can be said to be neither too bitter nor astringent.

Toraja Coffee Beans

Toraja coffee is a coffee bean that comes from the hilly areas of South Sulawesi. The high area around 1700m above sea level can be said to have good nutrition.

What's so special?

Latte

Latte or Caffè latte is espresso or coffee mixed with milk and has a thin layer of foam on top.

Brown Sugar

Palm sugar, or brown sugar, or kawung sugar is a sweetener made from the sap that comes from the male flower bunches of the palm tree.

Black Coffee

Black Coffee is the result of direct extraction from boiling coffee beans served without adding any flavours.
